Albert Einstein

The Genius of Physics

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a renowned theoretical physicist of German origin who made profound contributions to physics and is widely regarded as one of the greatest scientists in history.He developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. His work revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and energy. Einstein received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his discovery of the photoelectric effect, which contributed significantly to the foundation of quantum theory.

Scientific Achievements

  • Developed the theory of relativity
  • Published the mass-energy equivalence formula, E=mc²
  • Received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921
  • Contributed to the understanding of quantum mechanics
